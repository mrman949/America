Openings

Wear a collared shirt, typically white, tucked and well kempt.

Many wear an overcoat, with a tie, kept simple.

It's best to start with a firm handshake and introduction.

Speak plainly, your resume and cover letter tell much of
what your employer needs to know.
What you are there for is to prove your competence.

When the interview is over, the boss will tell you how you did,
and you should leave with dignity and optimism.
